Pizza

Description

Pizza is a popular Italian dish consisting of a leavened bread crust topped with sauce (typically tomato-based), cheese (like mozzarella), and various other ingredients, then baked at high temperatures. Its origins date back to ancient flatbreads, but the modern pizza evolved in Naples, Italy, in the 18th century as an affordable meal for the working class. Today, pizza is a globally recognized food with countless topping variations and is considered a comfort food.

Ingredients

  1. Yeast
  2. Sugar
  3. Bread Flour
  4. Olive oil
  5. Salt

Steps

  1. Let the dough come to room temperature

    2. After you're finished mixing the ingredients, allow your dough to rest on the counter for about 5 minutes. This will allow the gluten to relax, making the dough much easier to stretch and shape.

  2. Prepare Your Surface With Oil

    3. You might feel inclined to prep your workspace with a big handful of flour to prevent sticking. This is a helpful step with many kinds of dough. However, in this case, too much flour can make your pizza crust tough. Instead, rub your surface (and your hands) with a few tablespoons of olive oil and maybe a little bit of flour. This will prevent sticking, encourage a crispy texture, and ensure a gorgeous golden color.

  3. Shape the Dough

    4. You have a few options when it comes to shaping the dough. Stretch it in the air, use a rolling pin, or pat it with your hands. No matter which method you choose, make sure not to overwork the dough. Working it too much will create a tough texture.
