Pasta

Description

Lasagna is an Italian baked pasta dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with fillings such as ragù (meat and tomato sauce), béchamel (white sauce), cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan, and vegetables, then baking it until bubbly and browned. The dish can refer to both the layered baked casserole and the specific type of wide, flat pasta used to make it.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion
  • Canned Tomatoes
  • Fresh herbs
  • cheese
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasoning
  • Noodles
  • Eggs

Steps

  1. Cook the meat:

    2. Cook the ground meat in a skillet until browned and crumbly. Add the onion and continue cooking until it's translucent. Stir in the canned tomato products, half of the parsley, garlic, basil, 1.5 teaspoons of salt, oregano, and sugar.

  2. Cook the noodles

    3. Boil the lasagna noodles in lightly salted water until they're al dente.

  3. Make the cheese layer:

    4. Mix cottage cheese, Parmesan cheese, eggs, the remaining changed content, the remaining salt, and pepper in a bowl.

  4. Assemble the lasagna:

    5. Layer the ingredients according to the recipe (starting with sauce and ending with mozzarella) until the lasagna is assembled.

  5. Bake the lasagna:
